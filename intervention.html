Intervention

Noun

The action or process of intervening, especially in order to alter the outcome of a situation or to prevent something negative from happening.

Collocations:

  • To intervene in [a situation]
  • To stage an intervention
  • Government intervention
  • To call for intervention
  • Humanitarian intervention

Example Sentences:

  • "The government had to intervene in the crisis to prevent further escalation."
  • "The family staged an intervention to help their son overcome his addiction."
  • "Many argue that humanitarian intervention is necessary in conflict zones."
Verb

To become involved in a situation in order to change or prevent an outcome, typically in a way that alters the course of events.

Collocations:

  • To intervene in [a dispute]
  • To intervene to [do something]
  • To intervene directly
  • To intervene with [someone]

Example Sentences:

  • "The UN intervened in the conflict to protect civilians."
  • "The teacher had to intervene to stop the argument between the students."
  • "He intervened with the manager to discuss the issues at work."
