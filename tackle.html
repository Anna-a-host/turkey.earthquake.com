Tackle

Noun

A piece of equipment or gear used for a particular task, especially in sports or fishing. Can also refer to a challenge or problem that needs to be addressed.

Collocations:

  • To take on a tackle
  • Fishing tackle
  • Defensive tackle (in sports)
  • To have a tackle
  • To make a tackle

Example Sentences:

  • "He used his fishing tackle to catch the big fish."
  • "The defensive tackle made a huge stop on the quarterback."
  • "She had the right tackle for the job ahead."
Verb

To deal with or address a problem or task, or to physically confront someone, especially in sports.

Collocations:

  • To tackle a problem
  • To tackle a challenge
  • To tackle [someone] (in sports)
  • To tackle head-on
  • To successfully tackle

Example Sentences:

  • "We need to tackle the issue of climate change urgently."
  • "He tackled the opponent with incredible speed."
  • "They tackled the project head-on, finishing it ahead of schedule."
