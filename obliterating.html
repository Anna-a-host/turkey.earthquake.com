Obliterate

Verb

To destroy something completely so that nothing remains.

Collocations:

  • Completely obliterate
  • Virtually obliterate
  • Totally obliterate
  • Almost obliterate
  • Efforts to obliterate
  • Attempts to obliterate
  • Obliterate all traces
  • Obliterate evidence
  • Obliterate memories
  • Obliterate the past

Example Sentences:

  • "The hurricane virtually obliterated this small coastal town."
  • "The village was totally obliterated by the bomb."
  • "The fire completely obliterated all evidence of the crime."
  • "The dictator's regime sought to obliterate all opposition."
  • "The disease is capable of obliterating entire populations."
