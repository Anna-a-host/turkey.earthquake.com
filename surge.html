Surge

Noun

A sudden powerful forward or upward movement.

Collocations:

  • There has been a surge in [production, prices, profits]
  • Has been a surge of interest in
  • The recent surge in interest (for)
  • A sudden surge of [embarrassment, excitement, anger]
  • A surge of [people, customers, fans] came through the [door, turnstiles, gate]
  • A surge to get to the [front, door]
  • There was a surge for [photographs, autographs]
  • [A sudden, an unexpected] surge
  • Released a surge of water
  • The surge of the [waves, sea]
  • There was [an electrical, a power] surge
  • Damaged by the power surge
  • The power surge damaged the [TV, computer]
  • Protect your [computer, devices] against electrical surges

Example Sentences:

  • "He experienced a sudden surge of excitement."
  • "A great surge of emotion swept through him."
  • "The storm surge caused widespread flooding."
  • "A last-minute surge in ticket sales saved the show from closure."
