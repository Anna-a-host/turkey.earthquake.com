Toll

Noun

The negative effect or cost of something, especially in terms of loss or damage.

Collocations:

  • A heavy toll
  • A high toll
  • A death toll
  • The toll of war
  • Take a toll on [something]
  • Exact a toll
  • Pay a toll
  • Emotional toll
  • Physical toll
  • Economic toll

Example Sentences:

  • "The war took a heavy toll on the country's economy."
  • "The stress of the job began to take its toll on his health."
  • "The death toll from the earthquake has risen to 500."
Verb

(Of a bell) To ring slowly, especially to mark death or disaster.

Collocations:

  • A bell tolls
  • Church bells toll
  • The tolling of a bell

Example Sentences:

  • "The church bells tolled in mourning."
  • "A single bell tolled as the funeral procession passed."
