Grip

Noun

A firm hold or grasp on something, often used to describe physical control or influence over something or someone.

Collocations:

  • To have a grip on [something]
  • To get a firm grip
  • To lose one's grip
  • To strengthen one's grip
  • To maintain a grip on [something]
  • To tighten one's grip
  • To slip from one's grip
  • To regain one's grip
  • To have a good grip on [something]
  • To get a better grip on [something]

Example Sentences:

  • "He struggled to maintain his grip on the rope as he climbed the mountain."
  • "The company's grip on the market was beginning to weaken."
  • "She tightened her grip on the steering wheel as the car skidded on the ice."
  • "He regained his grip on the situation after the initial shock."
