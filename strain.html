Strain

Noun

A force or pressure that causes something to change shape or condition, often leading to tension or damage.

Collocations:

  • Physical strain
  • Emotional strain
  • Strain on [something]
  • Under strain
  • Strain of [something]
  • Intense strain
  • Constant strain
  • Excessive strain

Example Sentences:

  • "He felt the strain of carrying heavy boxes all day."
  • "The emotional strain of the situation was overwhelming."
  • "The company is under strain due to economic pressures."
Verb

To exert force or pressure on something, often resulting in tension or deformation.

Collocations:

  • To strain muscles
  • To strain [something] too much
  • To strain under pressure
  • To strain to [do something]
  • To strain one's voice
  • To strain a relationship
  • To strain a resource

Example Sentences:

  • "He strained his muscles lifting the heavy weight."
  • "The workers strained to finish the project on time."
  • "She strained her voice shouting across the room."
